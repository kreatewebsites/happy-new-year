Aquarius in Chinese Zodiac: Myths, Strengths, and Compatibility

Aquarius in Chinese Zodiac
The Chinese Zodiac, known as Shengxiao, is based on a twelve-year cycle, with each year in that cycle related to an animal sign. However, Aquarius is not an animal in the Chinese Zodiac; it is a sign from the Western Zodiac, which is based on the solar calendar rather than the lunar calendar of the Chinese system. The Western Zodiac's Aquarius is typically associated with those born between January 20 and February 18. In the Chinese Zodiac, individuals born in a year corresponding to Aquarius might be born under the sign of the Ox, Tiger, or Rabbit, depending on the year.
Myths and Folktales
There are no direct myths or folktales associated with Aquarius in the context of the Chinese Zodiac, as it is not part of that system. In Western astrology, Aquarius is often associated with the myth of Ganymede, the young prince of Troy whom Zeus brought to Olympus to serve as the cupbearer to the gods, symbolizing the dispensing of knowledge and insight.
Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths Weaknesses
  • Innovative and original
  • Intellectual and analytical
  • Humanitarian and altruistic
  • Independent and progressive
  • Friendly and sociable
  • Unpredictable and erratic
  • Detached and aloof
  • Stubborn and unyielding
  • Rebellious and contrarian
  • Emotionally distant
Motivations
Aquarians are motivated by their desire for knowledge, their need for personal freedom, and their drive to contribute to the betterment of society. They are often inspired by innovative ideas and are motivated to create change and progress within their communities and the world at large.
Compatibility with Other Signs
  • Most Compatible: Gemini, Libra, and Sagittarius
  • Least Compatible: Taurus, Scorpio, and Cancer
Romantic Relationships
In romantic relationships, Aquarians value independence and intellectual connection. They are attracted to partners who are open-minded and willing to explore new ideas. They may struggle with emotional intimacy, but once they form a bond, they are loyal and committed. Communication and shared interests are key to maintaining a strong relationship with an Aquarius.
Friendship
As friends, Aquarians are supportive and encouraging. They are often the ones who push their friends to think outside the box and are always ready to lend a hand for a good cause. They form bonds with others who share their humanitarian values and intellectual curiosity. While they may have many acquaintances, they take time to form deep friendships, often seeking out those who are not afraid to be different.
