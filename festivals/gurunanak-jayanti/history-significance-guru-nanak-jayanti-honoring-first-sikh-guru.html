The History and Significance of Guru Nanak Jayanti: Honoring the First Sikh Guru

History of Guru Nanak Jayanti

Guru Nanak Jayanti, also known as Guru Nanak's Prakash Utsav, celebrates the birth of the first Sikh Guru, Guru Nanak. This is one of the most sacred festivals in Sikhism. The festivities in the Sikh religion revolve around the anniversaries of the 10 Sikh Gurus. These Gurus were responsible for shaping the beliefs of the Sikhs. Their birthdays, known as Gurpurab, are occasions for celebration and prayer among the Sikhs.

Significance of Guru Nanak Jayanti

Guru Nanak Jayanti holds great significance in Sikhism as it marks the birth of Guru Nanak, the first Sikh Guru. Guru Nanak Dev Ji, as he is referred to, laid the foundation of Sikhism in the 15th century. His teachings, which promote equality, love, and service, are deeply ingrained in the Sikh way of life. The festival is a reminder of these teachings and is a day of reverence and devotion.

Honoring Guru Nanak

On Guru Nanak Jayanti, Sikhs honor their first guru by participating in various rituals and ceremonies. The celebration begins two days prior to the birthday with the Akhand Path, a 48-hour non-stop reading of the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy book of the Sikhs. On the day of the festival, a procession known as the Nagar Kirtan is organized. This procession is led by the Panj Pyaras (Five Beloved Ones). The festival concludes with a feast known as Langar, a community meal which is open to everyone, regardless of religion, caste, or gender.
