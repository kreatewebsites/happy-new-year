Traditional Sankranti Quotes: Blessings and Good Wishes

Quote Description Significance
"Til gud ghya, god god bola." This Marathi quote means "Eat sesame and jaggery and speak sweetly." Sesame and jaggery are traditional foods eaten during Sankranti and are believed to bring good luck. Speaking sweetly is a reminder to be kind and respectful to others.
"Uttarayanam samprapthyartham punyakalevathishtanam." This Sanskrit quote means "During the auspicious time of Uttarayanam, stay in a holy place." Uttarayanam is the period when the sun starts its northward journey, and it is considered an auspicious time for spiritual practices. Staying in a holy place during this time is believed to bring blessings and good fortune.
"Sankranti maduve, hosa varshada shubhashayagalu." This Kannada quote means "Sankranti is like a wedding, wishing you a happy new year." Sankranti is a time of new beginnings and is celebrated like a wedding. It is a time to wish others a happy new year and to start fresh with renewed energy and enthusiasm.

Sankranti is a festival that is celebrated across India with great enthusiasm and joy. It marks the beginning of the harvest season and is a time to give thanks for the abundance of nature. Traditional quotes for Sankranti are a way to express good wishes and blessings to others. These quotes often include references to traditional foods, such as sesame and jaggery, which are believed to bring good luck. They also emphasize the importance of being kind and respectful to others, as well as the significance of spiritual practices during this auspicious time. Whether in Marathi, Sanskrit, Kannada, or any other language, these quotes are a reminder of the joy and blessings that come with the Sankranti festival.
